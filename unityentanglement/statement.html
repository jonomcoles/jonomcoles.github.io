Completed in 1908, Frank Lloyd Wright's Unity Temple is one of the first non-industrial structures to feature rebar-reinforced concrete. In 2015 the Unity Temple Restoration Foundation ordered $25 million in repairs to remediate extensive cracking and surface deterioration.

In 2011 my 8th grade geometry teacher David Ross instructed his students to solve "brain teaser" puzzles during class. All of Ross' puzzles were constructed from two interlocked and identical twisted concrete nails. The puzzles were considered to be solved when the nails were separated.

This sculpture attempts to recreate the mangled figure of demolished rebar within the context of the deterioration of the Unity Temple by mimicking the form of David Ross' puzzles.
