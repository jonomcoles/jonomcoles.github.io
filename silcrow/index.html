Silcrow is an award-winning architecture firm based between Los Angeles, San Francisco, and Pittsburgh. We work through architectural service to produce thoughtful spaces and critically engage contemporary issues.

Our leadership has renowned international experience in practice at a range of scales between regional infrastructure and handheld objects. Silcrow’s work has been awarded by The US Dept. of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), The City of LA, The California Homebuilding Foundation, CityLAB UCLA, and UC Berkeley, among other institutions, and has been exhibited and published by organizations including the Guggenheim Foundation, the American Institute of Architects California, and several academic journals.



Leadership:
Jono Coles is a licensed architect in California and Pennsylvania. He received his Masters in Architecture at the University of California, Berkeley, where he was awarded the Branner and Stump Memorial Travel Fellowships, the Miller Thesis Award, and the Eisner Prize for creative excellence. Prior to independent practice, Jono practiced internationally on award-winning projects for firms including Foster + Partners, First Office, and ALTO. Intertwined with Silcrow, Jono’s academic research constitutes a critical engagement with the infrastructures of the American architectural profession, and their design contingencies. He has taught architecture at UC Berkeley and UCLA, and serves as a guest critic at several Universities.
