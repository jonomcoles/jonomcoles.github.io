Campus Energy Constellation

Exhibited in Hillman Library for the 2020-2021 school year, the Campus Energy Constellation serves as a dynamic representation of the University of Pittsburgh's energy usage. Housed in a pitch black enclosure, the installation consists of 80 lights, each representing a building on Pitt's campus. Each light is hung from the ceiling at a different height, correlating to its respective building's size. Each light's brightness fluctuates with the energy usage of its respective building. This project seeks to physicalize the complex network that comprises a large part of the University's climate footprint in an engaging manner. A video description can be found here.

This project is part of the 2020 Creative Arts Fellowship hosted by the University of Pittsburgh Honors College and was funded by the Year of Creativity Grant.
